Executive Overview
The Opportunity in Brief

AICrowdCare Inc. (AICC) is a Toronto-based AI company developing the world's first comprehensive real-time crowd safety command platform. The platform transforms event safety from reactive crisis response into proactive, data-driven prevention — integrating predictive AI modelling, real-time crowd density monitoring, AI-optimised EMS dispatch, augmented reality wayfinding, and automated insurance documentation into a single deployable layer.

AICC is raising CAD $3,000,000 seed funding for 10% equity (pre-money valuation: CAD $27M). Proceeds fund the Toronto headquarters (May 1, 2026), an operational MVP (May 15, 2026), and the anchor proof-of-concept deployment at the Calgary Stampede (July 3–13, 2026, 1.4M+ attendees). The Calgary deployment generates live operational data, insurance actuarial documentation, and the Series A narrative.

Three structural advantages differentiate AICC from every funded competitor: James Rodmell's operational deployment experience from two Rugby World Cups; Dr. Julian Yeomans' full-time commitment from York University's Schulich School of Business; and Stewart Doma's track record as a serial startup CFO with multiple successful exits.

Section 2
The AICrowdCare Platform

The platform comprises five integrated modules operating as a unified command layer — deployable in pre-event (planning/simulation), live-event (real-time operations), and post-event (reporting/insurance) modes. Hardware-agnostic: mobile, tablet, command centre, or smart glass.

ModuleTechnical Specification
1. Predictive AI EngineRuns billions of Monte Carlo simulations against: (a) demographic composition of attendees; (b) event type risk profiles; (c) weather threshold models; (d) venue geometry and crowd flow dynamics; (e) proprietary LEMON deployment data from two Rugby World Cups (2020–2022). Powered by Dr. Yeomans' stochastic optimisation framework.
2. Real-Time Command LayerLive crowd density heat maps from camera feeds, sensor arrays, and mobile device density sampling. AI anomaly detection flags density thresholds and crowd compression before human operators can perceive them. Unified command dashboard for event director, security chief, and EMS coordinator.
3. 6-Level Cascading TriageExtends standard 4-level START triage protocol to 6 levels, linked directly to AI dispatch. Dynamically updates resource allocation as new information arrives. Every action timestamped to GPS coordinates and personnel ID for post-event actuarial audit.
4. AR Wayfinding & DispatchAugmented reality navigation integrated with live crowd density data. Optimal routes to incident sites update dynamically as crowd conditions change. Compatible with RealWear HMT-1, Vuzix M400 smart glasses and mobile AR interfaces. Public wayfinding mode enables voluntary crowd distribution.
5. Insurance DocumentationAutomated audit-trail generation: every dispatch action, triage decision, crowd alert, and staff position logged with timestamp, GPS coordinates, and personnel badge ID. Post-event reports formatted to Lloyd's of London and Canadian insurance market requirements. Enables premium credits of 5–20% for deploying events.
Sources: ICS/NIMS Emergency Management Standards; NIST TN 2212 (2023); NFPA 1600; START Triage Protocol (Newport Beach Fire); RealWear and Vuzix integration documentation.

Stream 3: Insurance Savings Revenue Share
How It Works
  1. Event organiser deploys AICC for a full season or event.
  2. AICC documentation submitted to insurer post-season.
  3. Third-party actuarial firm quantifies premium reduction attributable to AICC controls.
  4. AICC receives 15–25% of the verified annual premium reduction as recurring revenue.
  5. Relationship compounds: each year of AICC data strengthens the case for larger credits.
